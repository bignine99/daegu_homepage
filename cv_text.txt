498 Construction Project Management. I or II; 3cr. Characteristics of Construction Industry; project organizations; the 
design and construction process; labor, material, and equipment utilization; cost estimation; construction pricing and

Appendix II. 박사학위논문초록 (Abstract of Ph.D. Thesis) 
 
Construction Information Database Framework (CIDF) 
for Integrated Cost, Schedule, and Performance Control 
 
 
Daegu Cho 
Under the supervision of Professor Jeffrey S. Russell 
at the University of Wisconsin-Madison 
 
 
- 
ABSTRACT 
 
In the information age today, information is getting more and more important. A project success is directly related 
to continuous access to accurate project information. A construction project involves an enormous amount of 
information becoming more complex, specific, and professional. Though a lot of effort including information 
models, information tools, and standards has been applied in order to overcome these challenges, poor 
communication and information chaos still remained as an unsolved assignment in the construction industry.  
The following fundamental problems are identified within the research scope; limited focuses to a specific 
problem and its partial solutions, lack of integrated information framework, a fixed and single-directional 
representation method, sophisticated applications, and lack of appropriate evaluation method. Then, an inevitable 
question remains: “how does an information system provide the right data to the right person at the right time to 
the right place?” 
The primary purpose of research is to provide a guideline on how to gather, record, track, share, and retrieve 
project execution data with an accurate, efficient, and consistent manner. Research proposes a Construction 
Information Database Framework (CIDF) that supports various management functions having multi-perspectives 
and multi-levels of detail with a single database structure. For an effective respond to the topic, the research adopts 
four different parts of methodology; a faceted structure composed of independent 5W1H information units (the 
structural part); semantic-rich, multi-functional, and multi-perspectives database (the semantic part); a familiar, 
easy-accessible, and flexible information system (the application part); and an appropriate methods to measure 
model’s practicability (the evaluation part). These parts of methodology are integrated into one predefined 
structure, CIDF.   
The CIDF comprises of multi-levels of faceted information structures including a WHAT, WHERE, WHEN, 
WHO, WHY, and HOW information unit. The CIDF allows information users to access multi-directional, multi-
dimensional, multi-levels of detail information with relatively small amount of overhead effort comparing with 
current information tools. The CIDF would contribute on enhancing information quality and reducing overhead 
effort in the domain of construction execution database.
